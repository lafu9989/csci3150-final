What is Universal Design?

Universal Design is an approach to the design process that strives to create designs accessible to a diversity of users, including users with a wide range of abilities.

A Universal Design approach considers the needs of the diverse range of abilities of users in order to create a design that meets the needs of as many people as possible, including people with disabilities.

- Howard Kramer, Universal Design for Digital Media (2014)

Though Universal Design is commonly associated with accessibility efforts, it is broader in scope than Accessible Design. Accessible Design focuses on making designs accessible to disabled users, while Universal Design strives to create designs that work well for everyone. This involves fundamentals of the design, such as usability, as well as designing for a range of abilities.

Universal Design recognizes that all users' abilities and needs are fluid and may change depending on the user's environment and life circumstance. While Accessible Design mostly benefits disabled users, a Universal Design approach tends to benefit all users, regardless of ability.

What does Universal Design mean on the web?

It's hard to imagine a life without the Internet; nearly everyone's daily tasks involve using the Internet in some way. When websites aren't accessible, it can be extremely detrimental to the quality of life of users.

By making websites which are accessible through a variety of means, web designers can ensure that their content reaches as many people as possible. This benefits disabled users, who will be able to access the website content in the format of their choice. But it also benefits all users who may temporarily be in situations where they cannot interact with a website as they normally would.

What about other forms of digital media?

While the Web is important, it is not the only form of digital media that exists. Notably, mobile devices such as phones and tablets are increasingly used to access digital content. These devices, while capable of accessing the Web, usually have applications that are installed to the device which provide various functionalities.

These can present unique challenges in designing for all abilities, but also unique opportunities. Apps can provide a more focused experience than a website and are typically used for different purposes. Understanding these differences can help make using mobile apps a more inclusive experience. Ultimately, the principles of Universal Design can be applied to websites, apps, and any other user interface for digital media. While the specifics of the implementation may differ, the overall goal should be the same: to provide a consistent experience for users across all abilities, environments, and experiences.
